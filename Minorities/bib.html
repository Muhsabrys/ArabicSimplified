How References Are Written in Arabic Research Papers

A practical guide in English, with Arabic examples. This page explains in-text citation, the bibliography, Arabic and English sources, and how to cite books, articles, theses, videos, websites, conference papers, and more.

Main idea

Arabic research papers usually do not use a special citation system that is unique to Arabic. Instead, they normally follow the same major citation systems used internationally, such as APA, Harvard, or in some humanities contexts, Chicago-style footnotes. What changes is the language and script, not the basic citation logic.

What stays the same

The core pattern remains the same as in English academic writing: cite the source inside the text, then give the full reference in a list at the end.

What changes

Names, titles, and publication details may appear in Arabic script or English script, depending on the language of the source and the department or journal style.

What you should always check

Universities, supervisors, and journals sometimes impose their own house style. Always follow the official guide if one exists.

Simple rule: Every source normally appears twice: once in the text, and once in the final reference list.

In-text citation

In-text citation in Arabic papers follows the same logic as in English. You cite the author and year, and if you quote directly, you usually include the page number as well.

Case Explanation Arabic example
One author Use the family name and year. (السعدي، 2021)
Author in sentence Mention the author in the sentence, then place the year after the name. يرى السعدي (2021) أن...
Direct quotation Add the page number. (السعدي، 2021، ص. 45)
Two authors Write both names. (الخفاجي والعتيبي، 2018)
Three or more authors In Arabic writing, many people use وآخرون after the first author. (الشمري وآخرون، 2022)
Organization as author Use the institution name instead of a personal author. (وزارة التعليم، 2020)
No date Use a no-date equivalent if required by your style guide. (العتيبي، بدون تاريخ)

Paraphrase

When you rewrite the idea in your own words, you still cite the source.

يشير الباحث إلى أن تنمية مهارات الكتابة تحتاج إلى تدريب مستمر (السعدي، 2021).

Direct quotation

When you quote the exact wording, include the page number.

"تعد الكتابة الأكاديمية مهارة مركبة تحتاج إلى وعي لغوي ومنهجي" (السعدي، 2021، ص. 45).

Bibliography / reference list

The final list usually contains the full details of every cited source. In many Arabic papers, this section is titled المراجع or قائمة المراجع.

Typical order of information

  1. Author
  2. Year
  3. Title
  4. Publication information
  5. DOI or URL when relevant

Sorting practice in Arabic

Many Arabic departments sort Arabic references alphabetically by family name. In practice, many writers ignore the definite article ال when alphabetizing. Local conventions may vary.

Important distinction: Some departments use references to mean only the sources actually cited, while bibliography may sometimes include additional works consulted. Many students use the two labels interchangeably, but your department may distinguish between them.
Basic Arabic book entry

This is the general idea for a book entry in an author-date style.

السعدي، محمد. (2021). كتابة البحوث العلمية. دار الفكر.
Basic Arabic journal article entry

Include journal title, volume, issue, and page range.

الشمري، نورة. (2022). فاعلية التعلم المدمج في تنمية التفكير الناقد. مجلة التربية والعلوم، 15(2)، 45-68.

What if you have English sources?

This is one of the most common questions. In Arabic research papers, English sources are often left in English, especially in the bibliography. However, some institutions allow or prefer adding an Arabic translation of the title, usually in square brackets.

Most common practice

Keep the English source exactly as it appears in English. This is especially common in linguistics, computer science, education, and the social sciences.

Smith, J. (2020). Language and Cognition. Oxford University Press.

Possible translated-title practice

Some writers keep the original title and add an Arabic translation in brackets. This can help Arabic readers, but only do it if your department accepts this practice.

Smith, J. (2020). Language and Cognition [اللغة والإدراك]. Oxford University Press.
Best practical advice: If your paper is in Arabic and the source is in English, keep the source information in English unless your university guide tells you otherwise.
Arabic in-text citation with an English source

You may keep the author name in Latin script if that is your department's practice.

(Smith, 2020)
Arabic sentence with an English source

Writers differ here. Some keep the English name, others transliterate it.

ويرى Smith (2020) أن اللغة ترتبط بالإدراك على نحو وثيق.

How to arrange Arabic and English references together

  • Many Arabic theses place Arabic references first, then foreign references.
  • Some journals prefer one combined alphabetical list.
  • Both practices exist, so the official style guide matters here.

How to cite different source types

Below are practical examples for the most common types of publications and media. The explanations are in English, and the examples themselves are in Arabic where appropriate.

BOOK Book with one author

Author, year, title, publisher.

السعدي، محمد. (2021). كتابة البحوث العلمية: دليل عملي. دار الفكر.
BOOK Book with two authors

Write both names in the order required by your style.

الخفاجي، أحمد، والعتيبي، راشد. (2018). استراتيجيات التدريس الحديثة. دار النشر الجامعي.
CHAPTER Chapter in an edited book

Include the chapter author, chapter title, editor, book title, page range, and publisher.

جروان، فتحي. (1999). تعليم التفكير: مفاهيم وتطبيقات. في أحمد الطويل (محرر)، التربية الحديثة (ص. 120-145). دار وائل.
ARTICLE Journal article

Include journal name, volume, issue, page range, and DOI if available.

الشمري، نورة. (2022). فاعلية التعلم المدمج في تنمية التفكير الناقد. مجلة التربية والعلوم، 15(2)، 45-68. https://doi.org/10.1234/example
THESIS Unpublished master's thesis

State the type of thesis and the university.

البلوي، عائشة. (2017). واقع الأنشطة المدرسية غير الصفية في مدارس البنات بمنطقة تبوك [رسالة ماجستير غير منشورة، جامعة تبوك].
DISSERTATION Doctoral dissertation

If it is unpublished, say so. If it is in a repository, add the repository information if required.

الحربي، خالد. (2020). تحليل الخطاب الإعلامي العربي [أطروحة دكتوراه غير منشورة، جامعة الملك سعود].
WEBSITE Website or webpage

For web sources, many styles ask for the access date, especially when the content may change over time.

العتيبي، راشد. (2021، 15 مارس). كيفية كتابة البحوث العلمية. موقع مساعد البحث. تم الاطلاع في 2 أبريل 2026، من https://example.com
VIDEO YouTube video or online lecture

Use the channel or person who uploaded the video as the author when needed.

جامعة القاهرة. (2023). مدخل إلى علم اللغة [فيديو]. يوتيوب. تم الاطلاع في 2 أبريل 2026، من https://youtube.com/example
PODCAST Podcast episode

Include the episode title and medium if your style guide asks for it.

بودكاست العربية. (2022، 10 يونيو). تطور اللغة العربية [حلقة صوتية]. تم الاطلاع في 2 أبريل 2026، من https://example.com/podcast
CONFERENCE Conference paper

The exact format varies, but the title of the paper and conference details are usually included.

حسن، أحمد. (2020). تحليل الخطاب في وسائل التواصل الاجتماعي. في أعمال مؤتمر اللغة العربية والاتصال، جامعة القاهرة، 122-138.
NEWSPAPER Newspaper article

Include the full date when available.

المطيري، خالد. (2024، 12 يناير). التحول الرقمي في الجامعات العربية. الرياض.
REPORT Institutional or governmental report

Use the institution as the author if no individual author is listed.

وزارة التعليم. (2020). تقرير تطوير المناهج للمرحلة الثانوية. وزارة التعليم.

Advanced and tricky cases

These are the cases that often confuse students because they are not always handled consistently across institutions.

No personal author

Use the institution, organization, or site name.

(وزارة التعليم، 2020)

No date

Use a no-date equivalent only if your style guide allows it.

(العتيبي، بدون تاريخ)

Secondary citation

Use this when you did not read the original source directly. In serious research, it is better to consult the original source whenever possible.

(ابن خلدون، كما ورد في السعدي، 2021)

Classical Arabic texts

Classical works may need edition details, editor names, volume numbers, and page numbers. Humanities departments often have their own preferred format here.

ابن جني. (1952). الخصائص (تحقيق محمد علي النجار). دار الكتب المصرية.

Footnotes instead of author-date citation

Some Arabic humanities papers, especially in literature, history, Islamic studies, and philosophy, may use footnotes rather than parenthetical in-text citation. In that case, the citation appears in a note at the bottom of the page, and the bibliography still appears at the end.

محمد السعدي، كتابة البحوث العلمية (بيروت: دار الفكر، 2021)، 45.

This is only an illustrative note-style example. Exact punctuation varies by style guide.

Common mistakes in Arabic research papers

These are the problems that appear again and again in student writing.

Mistake 1

Mixing citation styles in the same paper.

Example: using APA in some places and footnotes in others without a clear reason.

Mistake 2

Quoting directly without a page number.

Mistake 3

Adding a source to the bibliography that never appears in the text, or citing a source in the text but forgetting it in the final list.

Mistake 4

Changing the spelling of authors' names from one entry to another.

Mistake 5

Translating English titles inconsistently or inventing translations without indicating that they are translations.

Mistake 6

Using websites with incomplete information, such as no author, no date, and no clear title, without handling them carefully.

Good practice: Before final submission, compare every in-text citation against the final reference list line by line.

Quick templates you can reuse

These are simple templates you can adapt quickly when writing a paper.

Book template
اسم العائلة، الاسم الأول. (السنة). عنوان الكتاب. دار النشر.
Journal article template
اسم العائلة، الاسم الأول. (السنة). عنوان المقالة. اسم المجلة، رقم المجلد(رقم العدد)، الصفحات.
Thesis template
اسم العائلة، الاسم الأول. (السنة). عنوان الرسالة [رسالة ماجستير غير منشورة، اسم الجامعة].
Website template
اسم المؤلف أو الجهة. (السنة، اليوم الشهر). عنوان الصفحة أو المقال. اسم الموقع. تم الاطلاع في اليوم الشهر السنة، من الرابط
Video template
اسم المؤلف أو الجهة. (السنة). عنوان الفيديو [فيديو]. اسم المنصة. تم الاطلاع في اليوم الشهر السنة، من الرابط

Final practical advice

  • Choose one style and stay consistent.
  • Keep English sources in English unless your department requests otherwise.
  • Use Arabic examples naturally in the body of the paper, but keep source details accurate to the original publication.
  • Always check whether your department wants APA, Harvard, or footnotes.
This page is designed as a practical academic guide. It is intentionally written in English for explanation, while keeping examples in Arabic so the patterns are easy to reuse in actual Arabic research papers.

Traditional Dishes and Dining Customs

Staple Dishes

المزة - Mezze

Small appetizer dishes shared before the main meal. Includes hummus, baba ghanoush, tabbouleh, falafel, and stuffed grape leaves.

الكبسة - Kabsa

Gulf rice dish with meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices. Often served on large platters for communal dining.

المنسف - Mansaf

Jordanian national dish: lamb cooked in fermented dried yogurt, served over rice with pine nuts.

الكسكسي - Couscous

North African staple: steamed semolina grains served with vegetables and meat in flavorful broth.

Desserts & Sweets

البقلاوة - Baklava

Layers of phyllo pastry filled with nuts and sweetened with honey or syrup

الكنافة - Kunafa

Shredded phyllo or semolina with sweet cheese, soaked in sugar syrup

المهلبية - Muhallabia

Creamy milk pudding flavored with rose water or orange blossom

Beverages

القهوة العربية

Arabic coffee with cardamom

الشاي

Sweet mint tea

عصير التمر - Jallab

Date syrup drink with rosewater

Dining Customs

  • • Right Hand: Eating with the right hand is customary
  • • Communal Eating: Sharing from a central platter strengthens bonds
  • • Generosity: Hosts insist guests eat more, refusing once is polite
  • • Starting Phrase: "Bismillah" (In God's name) before eating
  • • Finishing: "Alhamdulillah" (Praise be to God) after meals
  • • Hospitality: Offering food to guests is sacred duty

Core Arab Values and Social Etiquette

Core Values

العائلة - Family

Family is the foundation of society. Extended families maintain close ties, often living nearby. Elders are deeply respected, and caring for aging parents is a sacred duty.

الكرم - Generosity

Hospitality and generosity are paramount. Sharing resources, feeding guests lavishly, and helping those in need reflect honor and nobility.

الشرف - Honor

Personal and family honor guide behavior. Reputation matters greatly. Actions reflect on one's entire family, making integrity essential.

الاحترام - Respect

Showing respect to elders, teachers, and authority figures is fundamental. Politeness and formal greetings demonstrate proper upbringing.

Social Etiquette

Greetings & Conversation

  • • Use formal titles (Doctor, Professor, Sheikh) with names
  • • Ask about family, health, and wellbeing extensively
  • • Rushing conversations is considered rude
  • • Maintain eye contact to show sincerity
  • • Speaking loudly may be seen as passionate, not aggressive

Dress & Modesty

  • • Modest dress is valued, especially in conservative areas
  • • Women often cover hair with hijab in many Arab countries
  • • Men typically wear long trousers, avoiding shorts in formal settings
  • • Traditional dress (thobe, abaya, kaftan) worn for special occasions

Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • ✓ Accept offered coffee/tea (refusing is impolite)
  • ✓ Use your right hand for eating and greeting
  • ✓ Remove shoes when entering homes
  • ✓ Stand when elders enter the room
  • ✓ Bring gifts when visiting homes
  • ✓ Ask permission before photographing people

Don't

  • ✗ Show the soles of your feet
  • ✗ Use your left hand for eating or passing items
  • ✗ Point with your finger (use whole hand)
  • ✗ Public displays of affection between couples
  • ✗ Criticize religion or politics openly
  • ✗ Refuse hospitality repeatedly

Communication Style

Arab communication values indirect approaches and preserving dignity. Saving face is crucial - direct refusals or criticism are often softened. "Inshallah" (God willing) and "Mashallah" (God has willed) are common expressions showing faith and avoiding presumption.

Common Phrases for Politeness

تفضل / تفضلي - Please (offering something)

بارك الله فيك - May God bless you (thank you)

على راحتك - Take your time / At your convenience

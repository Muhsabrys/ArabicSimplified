Celebrations, Rituals, and Social Customs

Arab Hospitality (الضيافة)

Hospitality is sacred in Arab culture. Guests are treated with utmost respect and generosity, often offered the best food and accommodation. This tradition stems from Bedouin desert culture where travelers needed shelter and sustenance.

Coffee Ceremony

Arabic coffee (قهوة) is served as a welcoming gesture, often with dates

Generous Feasts

Hosts prepare abundant food, insisting guests eat more

Open Homes

Welcoming strangers is a point of honor and pride

Wedding Traditions (الأعراس)

Henna Night (ليلة الحناء)

The night before the wedding, the bride's hands and feet are decorated with intricate henna designs. Female relatives and friends gather for music, dancing, and celebration.

Zaffa (الزفة)

A joyous wedding procession with drummers, dancers, and singers leading the bride and groom. The couple enters the venue with music and ululation (zaghareet).

Extended Celebrations

Weddings often last multiple days with separate events for men and women, featuring traditional music, dabke dancing, and elaborate feasts.

Greetings & Social Gestures

Handshakes & Kisses

Men shake hands, often placing the left hand over the heart. Close friends may exchange cheek kisses (usually 2-3). Women greet similarly among themselves.

Inquiring About Family

Asking about someone's family and health is expected and shows genuine care. Brief greetings are considered rude.

Removing Shoes

Shoes are removed when entering homes and mosques. This shows respect and maintains cleanliness.

Gift-Giving Customs

Gift-giving strengthens social bonds. When visiting someone's home, it's customary to bring sweets, dates, or flowers. Gifts are typically given with both hands and opened privately to avoid appearing greedy.

Common Occasions for Gifts

  • • Eid celebrations (عيدية - Eidiya: money given to children)
  • • Returning from Hajj or Umrah (souvenirs from Mecca)
  • • Weddings and engagements (gold jewelry, money)
  • • Birth of a baby (clothing, gold)
  • • Moving into a new home (household items)

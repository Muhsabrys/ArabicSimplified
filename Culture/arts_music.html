Traditional and Contemporary Arab Arts

Traditional Music

الطرب - Classical Arabic Music

Characterized by complex maqam (modal) systems, improvisation, and emotional expression. Legendary singers include Umm Kulthum, Fairuz, and Abdel Halim Hafez.

Oud & Traditional Instruments

The oud (lute), qanun (zither), ney (flute), and darbuka (drum) are essential. These instruments date back centuries and remain central to Arab music.

Visual Arts

الخط العربي - Arabic Calligraphy

The art of beautiful writing elevated to its highest form. Scripts include Kufic, Naskh, Thuluth, and Diwani. Calligraphy adorns mosques, manuscripts, and modern art.

Geometric Patterns

Islamic art uses intricate geometric designs symbolizing infinity and divine order. Found in architecture, textiles, and ceramics.

Miniature Paintings

Detailed illustrations in manuscripts depicting historical events, poetry, and scientific texts. Persian and Arab miniatures influenced each other.

Traditional Crafts

Carpet Weaving

Persian and Arab carpets with intricate patterns

Glassblowing

Syrian and Egyptian glass crafts

Metalwork

Brass and copper engravings

Modern Arab Arts

Contemporary Arab artists blend traditional techniques with modern themes. Arab cinema, particularly Egyptian, has been influential since the 1920s. Street art and graffiti emerged during the Arab Spring as forms of political expression.

Notable Modern Artists

Mahmoud Said (Egypt), Etel Adnan (Lebanon), and Shirin Neshat blend Arab heritage with contemporary art movements.

Festivals, Holidays & Celebrations in Arab Culture

Islamic Holidays

رمضان - Ramadan

Ninth month of Islamic calendar. Muslims fast from dawn to sunset, focusing on prayer, charity, and self-discipline. Nights filled with family gatherings, special foods, and Taraweeh prayers.

Traditions: Iftar (breaking fast), Suhoor (pre-dawn meal), decorative lanterns (فانوس), special TV shows, increased charity

عيد الفطر - Eid al-Fitr

Festival marking the end of Ramadan. Three-day celebration with special prayers, new clothes, gift-giving (عيدية - eidiyah), and feasting.

Traditions: Morning prayer, visiting family/friends, children receive money gifts, eating sweets (kahk, ma'amoul)

عيد الأضحى - Eid al-Adha

Festival of Sacrifice commemorating Prophet Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son. Coincides with Hajj pilgrimage. Families sacrifice an animal (sheep, goat, cow) and distribute meat to poor.

Traditions: Special prayer, animal sacrifice (qurbani), meat distribution, family gatherings, four-day celebration

المولد النبوي - Mawlid al-Nabi

Prophet Muhammad's birthday (12th Rabi' al-Awwal). Celebrated with poetry, religious lectures, and distributing sweets. Observance varies by region.

Regional & National Celebrations

Independence Days

Each Arab country celebrates its independence with parades, fireworks, and national pride displays. Schools and government offices close.

رأس السنة الهجرية - Islamic New Year

First day of Muharram marks new Islamic year. Quieter observance with reflection and religious gatherings.

Teacher's Day

Celebrated across Arab countries (dates vary). Students give flowers and gifts to teachers, honoring their role.

Mother's Day

March 21st in most Arab countries. Families honor mothers with gifts, flowers, and special meals.

Cultural Festivals

مهرجان جرش - Jerash Festival (Jordan)

Annual arts and culture festival in ancient Roman city. Features music, dance, theater, and poetry from across Arab world.

مهرجان دبي للتسوق - Dubai Shopping Festival (UAE)

Month-long retail and entertainment extravaganza. Fireworks, concerts, sales, and family activities attract millions of visitors.

مهرجان الجنادرية - Jenadriyah Festival (Saudi Arabia)

National heritage and cultural festival showcasing Saudi traditions: camel races, poetry, traditional crafts, and regional cuisines.

مهرجان قرطاج الدولي - Carthage International Festival (Tunisia)

Summer festival featuring music, theater, and dance from Mediterranean and Arab countries in ancient Roman amphitheater.

Celebration Customs

Food & Feasting

  • • Special dishes for each occasion (kahk for Eid, qatayef for Ramadan)
  • • Large family gatherings with abundant food
  • • Distributing food to neighbors and needy
  • • Traditional sweets and desserts

Social Traditions

  • • Visiting extended family and friends
  • • New clothes for holidays (especially children)
  • • Giving money gifts (عيدية) to children
  • • Decorating homes and streets with lights

Holiday Greetings

عيد مبارك - Eid Mubarak (Blessed Eid)

كل عام وأنتم بخير - Kul 'aam wa antum bikhair (May you be well every year)

رمضان كريم - Ramadan Kareem (Generous Ramadan)

تقبل الله منا ومنكم - Taqabbal Allahu minna wa minkum (May God accept from us and from you)

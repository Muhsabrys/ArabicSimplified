The Five Pillars of Islam

الشهادة - Shahada (Faith)

The declaration of faith: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger." This testimony is the foundation of Islamic belief.

الصلاة - Salah (Prayer)

Muslims pray five times daily facing Mecca: Fajr (dawn), Dhuhr (midday), Asr (afternoon), Maghrib (sunset), and Isha (night). Prayer connects believers to Allah.

الزكاة - Zakat (Charity)

Mandatory charity of 2.5% of one's wealth annually to help the poor. Zakat purifies wealth and promotes social equity.

الصيام - Sawm (Fasting)

Fasting during Ramadan from dawn to sunset. This develops self-discipline, empathy for the less fortunate, and spiritual growth.

الحج - Hajj (Pilgrimage)

The pilgrimage to Mecca once in a lifetime for those who are able. Millions gather annually, symbolizing unity and equality before God.

Sacred Texts

القرآن الكريم - The Quran

The holy book of Islam, believed to be the literal word of Allah revealed to Prophet Muhammad over 23 years. It contains 114 chapters (surahs) covering theology, law, morality, and guidance for life. The Quran is recited in Arabic worldwide and is the foundation of Islamic law (Sharia).

الحديث - Hadith

Collections of the Prophet Muhammad's sayings, actions, and approvals. Hadith literature complements the Quran and provides practical examples of Islamic living. The most authentic collections include Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim.

The Islamic Calendar

The Islamic calendar is lunar-based with 12 months of 29-30 days (354-355 days per year). It begins with the Hijra (Prophet Muhammad's migration from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE).

Important Islamic Months

  • Ramadan (9th month): Month of fasting, prayer, and reflection
  • Dhul-Hijjah (12th month): Month of Hajj pilgrimage
  • Muharram (1st month): Islamic New Year
  • Rajab & Sha'ban: Months of spiritual preparation

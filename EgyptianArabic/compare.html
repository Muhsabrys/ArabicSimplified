How Egyptians Make Comparisons

In Egyptian Arabic, comparisons are often straightforward. Here are some common ways to compare things, people, or qualities, along with interactive examples.

Common Comparison Structures

  • Comparative: "أفعل من" (af'al min) - More/Less than: Used for "more [adjective] than" or "less [adjective] than". The adjective takes a specific comparative form (often starting with 'أ'). Example: "أنا أطول منك" (Ana atwal mennak) - I am taller than you. Example: "هي أقل ذكاءً منه" (Heya aqall zaka'an mennoh) - She is less intelligent than him.
  • Equality: "زي" (zayy) - Like / As: Used for "as [adjective] as" or simply "like". Example: "هو ذكي زي أخوه" (Howa zaki zayy akhuh) - He is as smart as his brother.
  • Superlative: "الأفعل" (el-af'al) - The Most/Least: Often formed by adding "الـ" (el-) to the comparative form of the adjective. This expresses "the most [adjective]" or "the [adjective]-est". Example: "الأكبر" (el-akbar) - The biggest/oldest. Example: "الأسرع" (el-asra') - The fastest.

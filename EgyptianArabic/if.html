How Egyptians Use Conditional Sentences (If...Then)

1. Using "لو" (Law) for Hypothetical or Possible Conditions

The word "لو" is the most common way to express conditional sentences in Egyptian Arabic. It works like "if" in English.

لو خلّصت شُغل بدري، هاروح السينما.
If I finish work early, I’ll go to the cinema.
لو شُفت أحمد، بلّغه إن أنا اتصلت.
If you see Ahmed, tell him I called.
لو الجو بَرِّد، خُد جاكيت.
If it gets cold, take a jacket.

2. Using "إذا" (Iza) — Formal or Emphatic in Tone

The word "إذا" is used to express conditions too, especially in formal speech or when emphasizing certainty. It's not rare in educated or media contexts.

إذا نجحت، هتحتفل معايا؟
If I succeed, will you celebrate with me?
إذا اتأخرت، كل من غيري.
If I’m late, eat without me.
إذا احتجت حاجة، كلّمني.
If you need anything, call me.

3. Using "يبقى" (Yeb'a) in the Result Clause

Egyptians often say "يبقى" in the result clause. It’s like saying "then" or "that means..."

لو الجو حر، يبقى هنقعد في البيت.
If it’s hot, then we’ll stay home.
لو سافر، يبقى مش هييجي الحفلة.
If he travels, then he won’t come to the party.
لو ده التليفون بتاعك، يبقى ده شاحنك.
If this is your phone, then this is your charger.

4. Negative Conditions

To express a negative condition, use ما before the verb.

لو ما ذاكرتش، هتسقط.
If you don’t study, you’ll fail.
لو ما اتصلتش بيّا، هازعل.
If you don’t call me, I’ll be upset.
لو ما نزلناش دلوقتي، هنتأخر.
If we don’t leave now, we’ll be late.

📝 Notes

  • "لو" is the default for everyday speech.
  • "إذا" is used in formal settings, storytelling, or when emphasizing logic or certainty.
  • "يبقى" adds clarity to the result clause and is common in spoken discourse.

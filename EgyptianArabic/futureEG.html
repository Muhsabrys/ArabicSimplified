🇪🇬 الكلام عن المستقبل بالمصري

Speaking of the Future in Egyptian Arabic

Egyptian Arabic uses simple prefixes and particles to talk about the future, making it easy to learn. Let's see how it works!

✅ المستقبل الإيجابي (Affirmative Future)

The Main Rule: The "Will" Prefix هـ

The most common way to say something will happen is to add the prefix هـ (ha-) to the beginning of the present imperfect verb.

Formula: هـ + Present Verb

أنا بروح

(Ana barūḥ – I go)

+

هـ

(ha-)

=

أنا هروح

(Ana harūḥ – I will go)

Near Future Events

For things happening very soon, you can often just use the present tense with a time word like "tomorrow."

نتكلم في الموضوع ده بكرة.

We’re talking about this topic tomorrow.

Planned Actions (Active Participle)

Using the active participle (اسم الفاعل) shows a definite plan.

منى مسافرة الأسبوع اللي جاي.

Mona is planning to travel next week.

Intentions & Possibilities

Use words like عايز (want), ناوي (intend), or ممكن (maybe).

أنا عايز أسافر السنة الجاية.

I want to travel next year.

❌ المستقبل المنفي (Negative Future)

The Main Rule: The "Won't" Particle مش

To say something will not happen, simply place the particle مش (mish) before the future verb.

Formula: مش + Future Verb

مش

(mish)

+

أنا هروح

(Ana harūḥ – I will go)

=

أنا مش هروح

(Ana mish harūḥ – I won’t go)

Negating Near Future

To negate a future action using present tense with a time word, use مش before the future verb.

مش هنتكلم في الموضوع ده تاني.

We won’t talk about this topic again.

Negating Planned Actions

Just add مش (mish) before the active participle.

أحمد مش رايح بكرة السينما.

Ahmed is not planning to go to the movies tomorrow.

🌟 لمسة ثقافية (Cultural Insight)

You will almost always hear Egyptians add the phrase إن شاء الله (in shāʾ Allāh), meaning "God willing," after stating a future plan. This reflects the cultural and religious belief that the future is ultimately in God’s hands.

هروح الشغل بكرة إن شاء الله.

I will go to work tomorrow, God willing.

This simple system makes expressing future events in Egyptian Arabic accessible and easy to master!

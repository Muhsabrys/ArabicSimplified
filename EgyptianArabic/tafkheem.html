التفخيم والترقيق Rules of Emphasis (Tafkhim) and Softening (Tarqiq)

in Quranic Recitation

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the rules of Tafkhim (emphasis) and Tarqiq (softening) for Arabic letters in the recitation of the Quran. Understanding and applying these rules correctly is essential for proper Tajweed (the rules governing the pronunciation of the Quran).

1. Letters of Tafkhim (Emphasis)

Tafkhim is the act of pronouncing a letter with a full mouth, making the sound heavy and thick. This is achieved by raising the back of the tongue toward the palate.

a. Always Emphasized Letters (Huroof al-Istila)

There are seven letters that are always pronounced with Tafkhim, regardless of their vowel or surrounding letters. They are grouped in the mnemonic phrase:

خَصَّ ضَغْطٍ قِظْ

These letters are:

  • خ (kha): As in the word خَالِدُونَ (khalidun)
  • ص (ṣad): As in the word صِرَاطَ (ṣiraṭa)
  • ض (ḍad): As in the word ضَالِّينَ (ḍallin)
  • غ (ghayn): As in the word غَفُورٌ (ghafurun)
  • ط (ṭa): As in the word طَلَاقًا (ṭalaqan)
  • ق (qaf): As in the word قَالُوا (qalu)
  • ظ (ẓa): As in the word ظَلَمُوا (ẓalamu)

These letters have five levels of Tafkhim, from strongest to weakest, based on their vowel:

  1. Fatha followed by Alif (e.g., طَا): The strongest level.
  2. Fatha (e.g., طَ): Second strongest.
  3. Damma (e.g., طُ): Third level.
  4. Sukun (e.g., طْ): Fourth level.
  5. Kasra (e.g., طِ): The weakest level, though the letter is still emphasized.

2. Letters of Tarqiq (Softening)

Tarqiq is the opposite of Tafkhim. It involves pronouncing a letter with an empty mouth, making the sound light and thin. This is achieved by lowering the back of the tongue.

a. Always Softened Letters

Most of the Arabic alphabet falls into this category. These letters are always pronounced with Tarqiq. They are all the letters that are not among the seven Huroof al-Istila, the letter Lam in the name of Allah, or the letter Ra.

Examples include:

  • ت (ta): As in the word تُوبُوا (tubu)
  • س (sin): As in the word سَمِيعٌ (sami'un)
  • ب (ba): As in the word بِسْمِ (bismi)

3. Letters with Conditional Tafkhim and Tarqiq

Two letters, Lam (ل) and Ra (ر), can be either emphasized or softened depending on specific conditions.

a. The Letter Lam (ل)

The letter Lam is generally softened, but it is emphasized only in the name of Allah (الله) under certain conditions.

Tafkhim (Emphasis) Conditions:

  • The Lam in the name of Allah is emphasized if the letter immediately preceding it has a Fatha or a Damma.
    • Example with Fatha: قَالَ اللَّهُ (qal Allahu)
    • Example with Damma: هُوَ اللَّهُ (huwa Allahu)

Tarqiq (Softening) Conditions:

  • The Lam in the name of Allah is softened if the letter immediately preceding it has a Kasra.
    • Example with Kasra: بِسْمِ اللَّهِ (bismi Allahi)

b. The Letter Ra (ر)

The pronunciation of the letter Ra depends on its vowel and the vowel of the letter before it.

Tafkhim (Emphasis) Conditions:

  • If the Ra has a Fatha or a Damma.
    • Example with Fatha: رَبَّنَا (rabbana)
    • Example with Damma: رُحَمَاءُ (ruḥama'u)
  • If the Ra has a Sukun and the letter before it has a Fatha or a Damma.
    • Example: مَرْيَمَ (maryama)
  • If the Ra has a Sukun, the letter before it has a Kasra, and the letter after it is one of the seven always-emphasized letters.
    • Example: مِرْصَادًا (mirṣadan)

Tarqiq (Softening) Conditions:

  • If the Ra has a Kasra.
    • Example: رِجَالٌ (rijalun)
  • If the Ra has a Sukun and the letter before it has a Kasra, with the exception of the emphasized letters.
    • Example: فِرْعَوْنُ (fir'awnu)
  • If the Ra has a Sukun, and the letter before it has a Sukun (usually because of a stop), and the letter before that has a Kasra.
    • Example: بِكْرٌ (bikrun) (When stopping on the word)

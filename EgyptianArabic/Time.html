Telling Time in Egypt

An infographic on the language and culture of time, where ancient tradition meets modern life.

The Essential Questions

How to start a conversation about time.

To ask "What time is it?", you say:

الساعة كام؟

(Is-saa'a kam?)

To ask "When?" about an event, you use:

امتى؟

(emta?)

Anatomy of an Answer

Telling time follows a simple, logical structure.

Hour
↓
Connector
(و or إلا)
↓
Minutes

Time is expressed as: Hour + Connector + Minutes. The connector changes depending on whether it's 'past' or 'to' the hour.

Speaking the Time: Examples

It's all about fractions and key phrases, not just counting minutes. Here are some common examples:

1:05

واحدة وخمسة

(waHda wi-khamsa)

2:10

اتنين وعشرة

(itnein wi-'ashara)

3:15

تلاتة وربع

(talaata wi-roba')

4:20

أربعة وتلت

(arba'a wi-tilt)

5:25

خمسة ونص إلا خمسة

(khamsa wi-nuss illa khamsa)

6:30

ستة ونص

(setta wi-nuss)

7:35

تمانية إلا نص إلا خمسة

(tamanya illa nuss illa khamsa)

8:40

تسعة إلا تلت

(tis'a illa tilt)

9:45

عشرة إلا ربع

('ashara illa roba')

10:50

حدعشر إلا عشرة

(Hida'shar illa 'ashara)

11:55

اتناشر إلا خمسة

(itna'shar illa khamsa)

12:00

الساعة اتناشر

(Is-saa'a itna'shar)

The Cultural Clock

Minutes

Conjunctions in Egyptian Arabic Colloquial Speech

Conjunctions are the glue that holds sentences together, connecting words, phrases, and clauses. Mastering them is crucial for fluent and natural Egyptian Arabic conversation. Let's explore the most common conjunctions and how to use them.

مقدمة عن الروابط (Introduction to Conjunctions)

Conjunctions (روابط - rawābiṭ) help create complex and coherent sentences by showing relationships between different parts of your speech. They allow you to express sequence, cause, contrast, and more.

Below are some of the most frequently used conjunctions and linking phrases in Egyptian Arabic, along with clear examples.

روابط شائعة (Common Conjunctions)

و (wa) - And

The most basic conjunction, used to join words, phrases, or clauses.

أنا و أنت.

Ana wa inta. (Me and you.)

أكلت و شربت.

Akalt wa sharibt. (I ate and drank.)

فـ (fa-) - Then / So

Indicates a sequence of events or a consequence.

ذاكرت فـ نجحت.

Zākart fa-najaḥt. (I studied, so I succeeded.)

صحيت فـ فطرت.

Ṣaḥīt fa-faṭart. (I woke up, then I had breakfast.)

أو (aw) / ولا (wala) - Or

Used to present alternatives.

شاي أو قهوة؟

Shāy aw ʾahwa? (Tea or coffee?)

مش عايز ده ولا ده.

Mish ʿayez da wala da. (I don't want this or that.)

لكن (lākin) / بس (bas) - But

Used to introduce a contrast or exception.

هو غني لكن بخيل.

Howwa ghanī lākin bakhīl. (He is rich, but stingy.)

جيت بس متأخر.

Geet bas mitʾakhkhar. (I came, but late.)

عشان (ʿashān) - Because / In order to

Expresses reason or purpose.

جيت عشان أشوفك.

Geet ʿashān ashūfak. (I came because I wanted to see you / in order to see you.)

هو زعلان عشان خسر.

Howwa zaʿlān ʿashān khasir. (He is sad because he lost.)

لو (law) / إذا (izā) - If

Used for conditional statements.

لو جيت، هتيجي معايا؟

Law geet, hateegi maʿāya? (If you come, will you come with me?)

إذا وافقت، هتبدأ بكرة.

Izā wāfaʾt, hatibdaʾ bokra. (If you agree, you will start tomorrow.)

لما (lamma) - When

Indicates a temporal relationship.

لما أوصل، هتصل بيك.

Lamma awṣal, hatteṣal bīk. (When I arrive, I will call you.)

بنام لما أكون تعبان.

Banām lamma akūn taʿbān. (I sleep when I'm tired.)

إن (in) - That

Used to introduce a subordinate clause, often after verbs of saying, knowing, or thinking.

قال إن هو جاي.

Qāl in howwa gāy. (He said that he is coming.)

أنا عارف إن ده صح.

Ana ʿāref in da ṣaḥḥ. (I know that this is correct.)

بعد كده (baʿd kida) / وبعدين (wa baʿdein) - After that / Then

Indicates a sequence of events, often colloquial.

خلصت شغل، بعد كده روحت البيت.

Khallaṣt shoghl, baʿd kida roḥt el-bēt. (I finished work, after that I went home.)

أكلت، وبعدين نمت.

Akalt, wa baʿdein nimt. (I ate, then I slept.)

قوم إيه (ʾūm ʾeih) - And then what happened? (Narrative)

A colloquial phrase used to transition in storytelling, often implying a surprising or significant next event.

كنت ماشي في الشارع، قوم إيه، لقيت محفظة!

Kunt māshi fel shāreʿ, ʾūm ʾeih, laʾēt maḥfaẓa! (I was walking in the street, and then what happened, I found a wallet!)

حتى لو (ḥatta law) - Even if

Introduces a concession or a hypothetical situation that doesn't change the main point.

هروح حتى لو الجو وحش.

Harūḥ ḥatta law el-gaww waḥash. (I will go even if the weather is bad.)

مش هغير رأيي حتى لو زعلت.

Mish haghayyar raʾyi ḥatta law zaʿalt. (I won't change my mind even if you get upset.)

لِحدّ ما (liḥadd mā) - Until / إلّا لو (ʾilla law) - Unless

Indicates a time limit or an exception to a condition.

هستناك لِحدّ ما تيجي.

Hastannāk liḥadd mā tīgi. (I will wait for you until you come.)

مش هروح إلّا لو عزمتني.

Mish harūḥ ʾilla law ʿazamtnī. (I won't go unless you invite me.)

رغم كده (raghm kida) - Despite that / Nevertheless

Used to show a contrasting idea, similar to "however" or "even so."

كان تعبان، رغم كده كمل الشغل.

Kān taʿbān, raghm kida kammal esh-shoghl. (He was tired, despite that he continued working.)

بعدها بشوية (baʿdehā bi-shwayya) - A little while after that

Indicates a short time delay between two events.

وصلت البيت، بعدها بشوية نمت.

Waṣalt el-bēt, baʿdehā bi-shwayya nimt. (I arrived home, a little while after that I slept.)

أيا كان (ayyā kān) - Whatever / Whoever / Wherever

A general term for "no matter what/who/where."

أيا كان اللي حصل، أنا معاك.

Ayyā kān elli ḥaṣal, ana maʿāk. (Whatever happened, I'm with you.)

هروح أيا كان المكان.

Harūḥ ayyā kān el-makān. (I will go wherever it is.)

لحد دلوقتي (liḥadd dilwaʾti) - Until now / So far

Indicates a duration up to the present moment.

لحد دلوقتي مفيش أخبار.

Liḥadd dilwaʾti mafīsh akhbār. (Until now, there's no news / So far, no news.)

عشان كده (ʿashān kida) / وبالتالي (wibil-tāli) - Therefore / Consequently

Indicates a result or conclusion.

هو تعبان، عشان كده مش هيجي.

Howwa taʿbān, ʿashān kida mish hayigi. (He is tired, therefore he won't come.)

ذاكرت كويس، وبالتالي نجحت.

Zākart kwayyes, wibil-tāli najaḥt. (I studied well, consequently I succeeded.)

قُصر الكلام (ʾuṣr el-kalām) - In short / Long story short

Used to summarize or get straight to the point.

قُصر الكلام، احنا مش موافقين.

ʾUṣr el-kalām, iḥna mish mawāfʾīn. (In short, we don't agree.)

أول ما (ʾawwal mā) - As soon as

Indicates an immediate temporal sequence.

أول ما يوصل، قولي.

ʾAwwal mā yūṣal, ʾulīli. (As soon as he arrives, tell me.)

هبدأ شغل أول ما أصحى.

Habdaʾ shoghl ʾawwal mā aṣḥa. (I will start work as soon as I wake up.)

Using these conjunctions will help you build more complex and natural-sounding sentences in Egyptian Arabic!

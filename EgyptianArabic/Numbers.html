Core Principle: The "Units-First" Rule (21-99)

This is the most critical pattern: for all numbers from 21 through 99, Arabic reads the **Units** digit first, then the **Tens** digit, linked by the conjunction **"wa"** (و), meaning "and."

Pattern: Units + wa + Tens

Example: 74

أربعة وسبعون

Pronunciation: Arba‘ah wa sab‘ūn (Four and Seventy)

Group 1: Eleven to Nineteen (11 - 19)

These are special, single-compound words. They combine the unit digit (3-9) with the root word for ten (`‘ashar`).

  • 13 ثلاثة عشر Thalāthata ‘ashar
  • 16 ستة عشر Sittata ‘ashar
  • 18 ثمانية عشر Thamāniyata ‘ashar

Group 2: Twenty-One to Ninety-Nine (21 - 99)

The strict **[Unit] + wa + [Tens]** rule applies here. Notice how the tens digits (20, 30, etc.) are plural forms, ending in -ūn.

  • 37 سبعة وثلاثون Sab‘ah wa thalāthūn (Seven and Thirty)
  • 60 ستون Sittūn (Sixty)
  • 89 تسعة وثمانون Tis‘ah wa thamānūn (Nine and Eighty)

Reading Years (1900, 1992, 2010, 2024)

Years are structured by their place values, separated by **wa** (و). The order of reading is typically: **Thousands** $\to$ **Hundreds** $\to$ **Compound Number** (which itself follows the Units-First rule).

  • 1900

    ألف وتسع مئة

    Alf wa tis‘u mi’ah (A Thousand and Nine Hundred)

  • 1992

    ألف وتسع مئة واثنان وتسعون

    Alf wa tis‘u mi’ah wa ithnān wa tis‘ūn

    Order: (1000) $\to$ wa (900) $\to$ wa (2 and 90)

  • 2010

    ألفان وعشرة

    Alfān wa ‘asharah (Two Thousands and Ten)

  • 2024

    ألفان وأربعة وعشرون

    Alfān wa arba‘ah wa ‘ishrūn

    Order: (2000) $\to$ wa (4 and 20)

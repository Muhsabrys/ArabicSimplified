What is جملة الصفة?

In Arabic grammar, جملة الصفة (Jumlat As-Sifah) means “the adjective sentence,” or the relative clause in English.

It’s a complete sentence — verbal or nominal — that describes a noun.

In English, we say “the man who runs,” but in Arabic, we use a short sentence after the noun.

Golden Rule: Indefinite Noun

A sentence works as an adjective (جملة الصفة) only when the described noun (المنعوت) is نكرة (indefinite).

نكرة (Indefinite Noun) + جملة (Sentence)

If the noun were definite (معرفة), the sentence would just describe it, not act as an adjective.

Type 1: Verbal Clause (جملة فعلية)

رأيت طفلاً يلعب في الحديقة

I saw a child who is playing in the park.

قابلت رجلًا يعمل في المدرسة

I met a man who works at the school.

اشترينا كتابًا يشرح القواعد

We bought a book that explains grammar.

The green part (جملة الصفة) includes a pronoun that links back to the red noun (الضمير الرابط).

Type 2: Nominal Clause (جملة اسمية)

Here, the describing sentence starts with a noun or pronoun (a nominal sentence).

هذه قطة شعرها طويل

This is a cat whose hair is long.

رأيت بنتًا ملابسها جميلة

I saw a girl whose clothes are pretty.

The linking pronoun is inside the green clause, such as ـها or ـه, referring back to the red noun.

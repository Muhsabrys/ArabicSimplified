Arabic Grammar

Mastering المضارع المرفوع

A practical guide to the indicative present tense in Modern Standard Arabic, with a careful comparison to the spoken habitual present.

What does المضارع المرفوع mean?

المضارع is the present/imperfect verb. المرفوع means that the verb is in the indicative state. In beginner-friendly terms, this is usually the “normal” present-tense form when the verb is not affected by a word like أنْ, لن, لم, or لا الناهية.

Core rule

A present-tense verb is marfūʿ when no subjunctive or jussive particle comes before it. In MSA, the marfūʿ marker is usually either a final ḍamma or the retention of final ن.

MSA marker 1

Final ḍamma

يَدْرُسُ

He studies / is studying.

MSA marker 2

Keeping the final nūn

يَدْرُسُونَ

They study / are studying.

MSA vs. spoken Arabic: do not mix the systems

In MSA

The grammar marker comes at the end: أدرسُ, تدرسينَ, يدرسونَ.

In spoken Arabic

The habitual marker often comes at the beginning: بأدرس, بتدرسي, بيدرسوا.

After words like “want”

The next verb is not simply a main indicative verb: أريد أن أدرسَ in MSA, and عايز أدرس in spoken Arabic.

The General Idea & Transformation Flow

1. Strip the Present Marker

Most Arabic present-tense verbs begin with a starting prefix marker: أ، ن، ي، ت. These show who is doing the action. To start moving towards the past form, we always drop this prefix.

2. Examine the Remaining Stem

Once the starting prefix is removed, look at the base letters. Identify if they are normal consonants (Regular), contain a glottal stop (Hamza), have a merged double letter, or contain weak vowels (و ، ا ، ي).

3. Rebuild the Past Form

Apply the correct matching vowels or restore any dropped starting letters (such as restoring a dropped و in Weak-Start verbs). Complete the verb by appending the standard past ending (like ـتُ for 'I').

Key Lesson Takeaway

Forming the past tense is not just about deleting the present tense prefix. While removing the prefix helps you see the basic stem, the real key is identifying the verb class. Regular verbs stay completely stable, weak-middle verbs change internally, weak-end verbs change at their suffix, and complex prefixes must follow their specific structural templates.

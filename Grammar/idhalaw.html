Mastering Arabic "If"

Understanding the difference between إذا (Idha) and لو (Law)

In English, we mostly just use the word "if". But in Arabic, things get a bit more specific. The word you choose depends entirely on how likely the "if" scenario is to actually happen. Let's break down the two most common ones used in everyday spoken Arabic.

إذا

Idha (إذا)

The "Real" If / "When"

When to use it:

Use إذا when talking about a possible, likely, or real condition in the future. It's often closer in meaning to "when" or "whenever" because you expect the condition to be met.

Rule of thumb: If you can replace "if" with "when" in English and it still makes sense, use إذا.

Everyday Examples:

إذا رأيتَ أحمد، فأخبرهُ أن يتصل بي.

Idha ra'ayta Ahmad, fa-akhbirhu an yattasila bi.

If (when) you see Ahmad, tell him to call me.

Context: You expect they will probably see Ahmad.

إذا أنهيتُ عملي مبكراً، سنذهب لتناول العشاء.

Idha anhaytu 'amali mubakkiran, sanadhhabu li-tanawul al-'asha'.

If I finish work early, we'll go eat dinner.

Context: Finishing early is a real possibility today.

إذا احتجتَ إلى مساعدة، فأخبرني.

Idha ihtajta ila musaa'ada, fa-akhbirni.

If you need help, let me know.

Context: A standard offer, assuming they might need help.

لو

Law (لو)

The "Unreal" / "Imaginary" If

When to use it:

Use لو for hypothetical, impossible, or highly unlikely conditions. It's used for daydreaming, expressing regret about the past, or talking about things that aren't true right now.

Rule of thumb: If you are saying "If I were you..." or "If I had a million dollars..." or "If I had known yesterday...", use لو.

Everyday Examples:

لو كان لديَّ مال، لاشتريتُ سيارةً جديدة.

Law kana ladayya maal, la-ishtaraytu sayyaratan jadida.

If I had money, I would have bought a new car.

Context: I don't currently have the money. It's hypothetical.

لو كنتُ مكانك، لما ذهبت.

Law kuntu makanaka, lama dhahabtu.

If I were in your place (If I were you), I wouldn't go.

Context: Impossible condition; I cannot actually be you.

لو عرفتُ أنك مريض، لزرتُك.

Law araftu annaka mareed, la-zurtuka.

If I had known you were sick, I would have visited you.

Context: Regret about the past. I didn't know, so I didn't visit.

Quick Summary

Word Type of "If" English Vibe
إذا Possible, likely, future plans. "If/When" (I expect it could happen)
لو Impossible, hypothetical, past regrets. "If only / If I were" (It's just imagination)

Keep practicing! The context usually gives away which one you need.

I. Three States of the Present Tense Verb

The non-past verb (المضارع) appears in three grammatical cases, each triggered by specific preceding particles.

المرفوع (Al-Marfu')

Indicative

Default state; ongoing/habitual or general statements.

Markers: Damma (ـُ) or retention of Nūn.

يَشْرَبُ / يَلْعَبُونَ

المنصوب (Al-Mansub)

Subjunctive

After certain particles (e.g., أنْ, لَنْ, كَيْ), often purpose or negative future.

Markers: Fatḥa (ـَ) or deletion of Nūn.

أَنْ يَشْرَبَ / لَنْ يَلْعَبُوا

المجزوم (Al-Majzūm)

Jussive

After jussive particles (e.g., لَمْ), used for past negation or prohibitions with لا الناهية.

Markers: Sukūn (ـْ) or deletion of Nūn.

لَمْ يَشْرَبْ / لا تَلْعَبُوا

II. Main Subjunctive Particles (أدوات النصب)

These particles precede the Present Tense verb and put it in the Subjunctive (المنصوب) case.

أَنْ

(that / to) – Masdariyya

Primary linker between two verbs.

أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَذْهَبَ.

لَنْ

(will not)

Emphatic negation of future actions.

لَنْ أُسَافِرَ غَدًا.

كَيْ

(in order to)

Expresses purpose.

أَدْرُسُ كَيْ أَنْجَحَ.

لامُ التَّعليل (لِـ)

(for / in order to)

Also purpose; takes المصدر after nouns, or may precede a verb (then the verb is usually منصوب: لِأَتَعَلَّمَ).

جِئْتُ لِأَتَعَلَّمَ.

حَتّى

(until / so that)

Marks an end-point or goal; verb after it is often منصوب when indicating purpose.

سَأَنْتَظِرُ حَتّى يَعُودَ.

III. Subordinate Verb Forms & Structures

Two common ways to express subordinate actions (similar to English “to do / doing”):

Structure A: أنْ + المضارع (منصوب)

Very common with matrix verbs like “want, can, like, refuse”.

أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَذْهَبَ.

Structure B: المصدر (Masdar)

The verbal noun can replace a whole clause. With purpose, it can follow لِـ (e.g., لِلنَّجاحِ).

أُرِيدُ الذَّهَابَ.
أَدْرُسُ لِلنَّجَاحِ.

IV. Conjugation Rules: How the Verb Changes

The Subjunctive replaces the Indicative ending.

Rule 1: Standard Verbs (final vowel)

Singular forms: final ـُ → ـَ.

Indicative (Marfu')

يَكْتُبُ

→

Subjunctive (Mansub)

لَنْ يَكْتُبَ

Rule 2: The Five Verbs (حذف النون)

Dual, masculine plural, 2nd person feminine singular in -ينَ: delete the final Nūn.

Indicative (Marfu')

يَكْتُبُونَ

→

Subjunctive (Mansub)

لَنْ يَكْتُبُوا

The Nūn is removed.

Rule 3: Plural Feminine (نون النسوة)

Forms with نون النسوة are indeclinable (مبني)—no change across cases.

Indicative (Marfu')

يَكْتُبْنَ

→

Subjunctive (Mansub)

لَنْ يَكْتُبْنَ

V. Infinitive/Subjunctive Equivalence

أنْ + المضارع المنصوب often alternates with the المصدر. With purpose, لِـ can introduce the Masdar (e.g., لِلنَّجاحِ).

CRITICAL EXCEPTION: Verbs of Beginning

Do not use أنْ or لِـ after verbs of beginning/continuation (e.g., بدأ, أخذ, ظلّ).

بدأنا نَدْرُسُ اللغةَ العربيةَ.

The subordinate verb remains Indicative (مرفوع).

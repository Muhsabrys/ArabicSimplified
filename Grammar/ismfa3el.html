01

Learning Objectives

Morphology

  • Identify the active-participle pattern used with triliteral verbs.
  • Form the active participle from non-triliteral verbs.
  • Handle hamzated, doubled, and weak verbs correctly.

Syntax and Usage

  • Apply gender and number agreement.
  • Identify its syntactic position and case.
  • Explain when it governs a subject and a direct object like its verb.
Final goal: form the active participle accurately and use it in a natural, grammatically precise sentence.
02

What Is اسم الفاعل?

The active participle is a derived noun that identifies the person or entity performing an action, experiencing a state, or characteristically associated with an action.

Actual performer

كاتب الرسالة

The person who wrote or is writing.

Description or state

الطفل نائم.

A state occurring now or in a specified context.

Profession or role

هو مُدرِّس.

The derived form has become a common occupational noun.

The active participle is not an independent tense. Its time reference is inferred from context: now, tomorrow, habitually, or through the surrounding construction.
03

Core Formation Rules

Triliteral verb

فَعَلَ ← فاعِل

كَتَبَ ← كاتِب

فَتَحَ ← فاتِح

Non-triliteral verb

Imperfect stem ← initial مُـ + kasra on the penultimate consonant

يُدَرِّسُ ← مُدَرِّس

يَفْتَتِحُ ← مُفْتَتِح

Do not apply the pattern فاعِل directly to every verb. It is primarily the pattern for the basic triliteral verb.

05

Hamzated Triliteral Verbs

The pattern remains فاعِل, but the written form of hamza changes according to its position and surrounding vowels.

Initial hamza

أَخَذَ ← آخِذ

أَكَلَ ← آكِل

Two hamzas come together, so they are represented with madda: آ.

Medial hamza

سَأَلَ ← سائِل

رَأَسَ ← رائِس

The hamza follows the alif and is normally written on a yāʾ seat.

Final hamza

قَرَأَ ← قارِئ

بَدَأَ ← بادِئ

It is written on a yāʾ seat because the preceding consonant carries a kasra.

Key example: أَخَذَ ← آخِذ, not أاخذ or أخاذ.
06

Hollow Verbs: A Weak Medial Root Consonant

In many hollow verbs, the medial weak consonant appears as hamza on a yāʾ seat in the active participle.
Verbاسم الفاعلExample
قالَقائِلقال قائلٌ: الصدقُ نجاةٌ.
باعَبائِعالبائعُ يعرضُ السلعَ.
نامَنائِمالطفلُ نائمٌ.
صامَصائِمالرجلُ صائمٌ اليومَ.
قادَقائِدالقائدُ مسؤولٌ عن فريقه.
خافَخائِفالطفلُ خائفٌ من الصوت.
In standard Arabic, the active participles of قال and باع are قائل and بائع, not قاول and بايع.
07

Defective Verbs: A Final Weak Root Consonant

Basic forms

دعاداعٍ
رمىرامٍ
سعىساعٍ
قضىقاضٍ
هدىهادٍ

Rule for the final yāʾ

  • When indefinite and nominative or genitive, the final yāʾ is omitted and tanwīn kasr is used: داعٍ، قاضٍ.
  • In the accusative, the yāʾ appears: رأيتُ داعيًا، قابلتُ قاضيًا.
  • With the definite article, the yāʾ appears: الداعي، القاضي.
  • In a construct phrase, the yāʾ appears: داعي الخير، قاضي المحكمة.
هذا داعٍ إلى الخير.
but
رأيتُ داعيًا إلى الخير.
08

Doubled Verbs

When the second and third root consonants are identical, gemination appears in the active participle.

مَدَّ ← مادّ

مادٌّ يدَه.

رَدَّ ← رادّ

رادٌّ الأمانةَ.

عَدَّ ← عادّ

عادٌّ النقودَ.

مَرَّ ← مارّ

مارٌّ بالطريق.

Underlying morphological form: مَدَدَ ← مادِد ← مادّ after assimilation
The shadda represents two identical consonants; neither consonant is absent from the morphological analysis.
09

Assimilated and Lafif Verbs: Verbs Containing و or ي

Assimilated verb: the first root consonant is weak

وَعَدَواعِد
وَجَدَواجِد
وَقَفَواقِف
وَصَفَواصِف

It normally follows فاعِل without major changes.

Lafif verb: two root consonants are weak

وَقَىواقٍ
وَفَىوافٍ
رَوَىراوٍ
طَوَىطاوٍ

The فاعِل pattern combines with the rule for the defective ending.

Active Participle from a Non-Triliteral Verb

Start with the imperfect form, replace its imperfect prefix with مُـ, and place a kasra on the penultimate consonant.

1. Begin with the imperfect

يُدَرِّسُ

2. Replace the imperfect prefix

مُدَرِّسُ

3. Add a kasra to the penultimate consonant

مُدَرِّس

دَرَّسَ ← يُدَرِّسُ ← مُدَرِّس اِفْتَتَحَ ← يَفْتَتِحُ ← مُفْتَتِح

Agreement in the Sentence

When used attributively

رأيتُ طالبًا حاملًا حقيبةً.

مررتُ بطالبتين حاملتين كتبًا.

جاءت الطالباتُ مستعدّاتٍ.

When used predicatively

الطالبُ مُشارِكٌ.

الطالبتان مُشارِكتان.

الطلابُ مُشارِكون.

Correct formation is not sufficient. When the participle is an adjective, it must also agree with the noun in gender, number, definiteness, and case.

18

Time Reference and Context

Present situation

الطالبُ قارئٌ الكتابَ الآن.

The event is in progress now.

Future reference

أنا مسافرٌ غدًا.

The time expression establishes future reference.

Habit or characteristic

هو كاتبٌ معروفٌ.

A profession or recurring characteristic rather than only a momentary event.

The active participle can refer to a completed event in descriptive contexts, especially with أل or in a construct phrase. Its syntactic government, however, depends on definiteness, structural support, and time reference.
19

When the Active Participle Governs Like Its Verb

The active participle may function like an imperfect verb: it assigns nominative case to its subject and, when derived from a transitive verb, accusative case to its object.

Intransitive verb

أمُسافرٌ أخوك؟

مسافر: An active participle functioning as a governor.

أخوك: The nominative subject governed by the active participle.

Transitive verb

محمدٌ قارئٌ الكتابَ.

قارئ: An active participle functioning as a governor.

ضمير مستتر: Its understood subject, interpreted as هو.

الكتابَ: An accusative direct object.

يقرأُ محمدٌ الكتابَ ↔ محمدٌ قارئٌ الكتابَ
20

When the Active Participle Has the Definite Article أل

When the active participle is introduced by أل, it may govern like its verb without requiring a separate supporting construction.
  • جاء الكاتِبُ الرسالةَ. الكاتب is the sentence subject, and الرسالة is the direct object governed by the active participle.
  • أكرمتُ المُعلِّمَ الطلابَ. المعلّم is the object of the main verb, while الطلاب is the object governed by the active participle.
  • هذا هو المُنظِّمُ المؤتمرَ. المؤتمر is the direct object governed by the active participle.
These examples illustrate classical grammatical government. In contemporary usage, a construct phrase or a finite verb may be more natural in some contexts.
21

When the Active Participle Is Indefinite

An indefinite active participle normally governs when it refers to the present or future and is structurally supported by an element before it.
Type of Supportالمثال
Predicate of a subjectالطالبُ قارئٌ الكتابَ الآن.
Negationما تاركٌ المجتهدُ عملَه.
Questionأَحافظٌ أخوك القصيدةَ؟
Modification of an indefinite nounرأيتُ رجلًا حاملًا حقيبةً.

For classroom analysis, ask three questions: Is the active participle indefinite? Does it refer to the present or future? What preceding structure supports it?
22

The Active Participle in a Construct Phrase

محمدٌ قارئٌ الكتابَ.
The active participle governs directly, and الكتاب is accusative.
or
محمدٌ قارئُ الكتابِ.
قارئ is the first term of the construct, and الكتاب is formally genitive.

With direct government

  • Tanwīn remains on the active participle: قارئٌ.
  • The direct object is accusative: الكتابَ.
  • The construction closely parallels the meaning of an imperfect verb.

In the construct state

  • Tanwīn is removed: قارئُ.
  • The following noun is formally genitive: الكتابِ.
  • The construction may emphasize a stable relationship or association.
23

When the Active Participle Becomes a Lexical Noun

Some active participles are used so frequently that they become conventional nouns for professions, roles, or established categories.

طالِب

Originally one who seeks knowledge; now the conventional noun for a student.

سائِق

A common occupational noun.

حاكِم

A noun denoting a political or administrative role.

كاتِب

It may identify someone writing now or a person whose profession is writing.

Context determines whether the form is a governing participle, an adjective, or a partly lexicalized profession noun.

A Quick Formation Checklist

Question 1: Is the verb triliteral?

Yes: Begin with the pattern فاعِل.

No: Begin with the imperfect, replace the prefix with مُـ, and add a kasra to the penultimate consonant.

Question 2: Does the root contain hamza or a weak consonant?

Check whether the verb is hamzated, hollow, defective, doubled, assimilated, or lafif.

Apply the appropriate spelling or phonological adjustment.

Question 3: What gender and number are required?

Form the feminine, dual, or plural as required.

Question 4: What is its syntactic position?

Assign the correct case, then determine whether it governs like its verb.

Verb → verb type → formation rule → agreement → syntactic position → contextual meaning

Practice 4: Syntactic Government

Identify the active participle, then identify the subject or object that it governs:

  1. أَحافِظٌ أخوك القصيدةَ؟
  2. الطالبُ قارئٌ الكتابَ الآن.
  3. رأيتُ رجلًا حاملًا صندوقًا.
  4. يا طالبًا العلمَ، اصبر.
  5. هذا هو المُنظِّمُ الحفلَ.
1. حافظ is the active participle; أخوك is its subject; القصيدة is its direct object.
2. قارئ is the active participle; its subject is understood; الكتاب is its direct object.
3. حاملًا is the active participle; its subject is understood; صندوقًا is its direct object.
4. طالبًا is the active participle; its subject is understood; العلم is its direct object.
5. المنظّم is a definite active participle; الحفل is its direct object.

Practice 6: Language Production

  • an active participle from a defective verb;

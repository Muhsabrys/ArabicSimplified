الجملة الاسمية: الخبر المقدَّم لوصف الأماكن

Lesson focus: using fronted predicates to describe places in Arabic.

1. Lesson Goal

In this lesson, you will learn how to describe places in Arabic by saying what exists in them. This structure is very common when describing a house, an apartment, a classroom, a campus, a city, or a neighborhood.

Main idea: When Arabic describes what exists in a place, the sentence often begins with the place expression.

في + المكان + اسم
In / at a place + there is / there are something

في البيتِ غرفةٌ كبيرةٌ.

There is a big room in the house.

2. Grammar Explanation

A normal nominal sentence often begins with the subject:

الغرفةُ كبيرةٌ.

The room is big.

But when we want to describe a place, we often begin with the place expression:

في البيتِ غرفةٌ كبيرةٌ.

In the house, there is a big room.

الجزء الوظيفة الشرح
في البيتِ الخبر المقدَّم جاء في بداية الجملة، ويخبرنا عن المكان.
غرفةٌ كبيرةٌ المبتدأ المؤخَّر الشيء الموجود في المكان.

Important: In English, this often sounds like “There is” or “There are.”

في الشقّةِ مطبخٌ صغيرٌ.

There is a small kitchen in the apartment.

3. The Basic Pattern

شبه جملة / مكان + مبتدأ مؤخر
Arabic Pattern English Meaning
في البيتِ غرفةٌ. There is a room in the house.
أمامَ الجامعةِ مطعمٌ. There is a restaurant in front of the university.
بجانبِ المكتبةِ مقهى. There is a café next to the library.
خلفَ المدرسةِ ملعبٌ. There is a playground behind the school.
على الطاولةِ كتابٌ. There is a book on the table.

4. Useful Place Expressions

Use these expressions to begin your sentence.

Arabic English Example

على on على المكتبِ كمبيوترٌ.

6. Important Grammar Notes

After Prepositions

After prepositions such as في، على، من، إلى، بجانب, the noun is usually genitive.

في البيتِ

على الطاولةِ

بجانبِ المكتبةِ

The Delayed Subject

The noun after the place expression is usually nominative because it is the delayed subject.

في البيتِ غرفةٌ.

أمامَ الجامعةِ مطعمٌ.

في المدينةِ متحفٌ.

Do not confuse these two structures:

Sentence Meaning Focus
الغرفةُ في البيتِ. The room is in the house. We are talking about the room.
في البيتِ غرفةٌ. There is a room in the house. We are describing the house.

14. Quick Review

Use this structure when describing what exists in a place:

في البيتِ غرفةٌ.
أمامَ الجامعةِ مطعمٌ.
بجانبِ المكتبةِ مقهى.
Question Answer
What comes first? The place expression: في البيتِ، أمامَ الجامعةِ، بجانبِ المكتبةِ
What comes second? The thing that exists in the place: غرفةٌ، مطعمٌ، مقهى
What does it often mean in English? There is / There are

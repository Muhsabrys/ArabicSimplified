Nominal and Verbal Sentences in Arabic

This page explains the difference between الجملة الاسمية and الجملة الفعلية. The main point is word order: Arabic sentences may begin with a noun or with a verb, and this affects verb agreement.

1What is a nominal sentence?

A جملة اسمية is a sentence that begins with a noun. It usually consists of a مبتدأ and a خبر.

Noun / Subject → Predicate / Information
الطُلّابُ في الصَّفِّ. The students are in the classroom.
الحكومةُ الجديدةُ مهمّةٌ. The new government is important.

In many nominal sentences, there is no visible verb equivalent to English “is” or “are.”

2What is a verbal sentence?

A جملة فعلية is a sentence that begins with a verb. It usually consists of a verb followed by a subject.

Verb → Subject → Rest of the sentence
دخلَ الطُلّابُ الصَّفَّ. The students entered the classroom.
سافرتْ مريمُ إلى القاهرة. Maryam traveled to Cairo.

In a verbal sentence, the verb comes first. If the subject is written separately after the verb, the verb remains singular.

The basic difference in word order

Sentence type Word order Example Meaning
جملة اسمية Noun + verb/predicate الطُلّابُ دخلوا الصَّفَّ. The students entered the classroom.
جملة فعلية Verb + subject دخلَ الطُلّابُ الصَّفَّ. The students entered the classroom.

Important note

The two Arabic sentences above have a similar meaning, but the grammar is different. In a جملة اسمية, the noun comes first, so the verb agrees with it in number and gender. In a جملة فعلية, the verb comes first, so the verb stays singular when the subject follows it.

Subject–verb agreement

1

When the verb comes before the subject, the verb is singular.

In a جملة فعلية, when the verb precedes the subject, the verb remains singular, even if the subject is dual or plural.

سافرَ الأصدقاءُ إلى تونس. Correct: سافرَ is singular because it comes before the subject.
حضرتِ الطالباتُ المحاضرةَ. Correct: the verb is feminine singular because الطالبات is a human feminine plural.
2

When the subject comes before the verb, the verb agrees with the subject.

In a جملة اسمية, if the sentence begins with the subject and the verb follows it, the verb should agree with the subject in number and gender.

الأصدقاءُ سافروا إلى تونس. Correct: plural subject before the verb → plural verb.
الطالباتُ حضرنَ المحاضرةَ. Correct: feminine plural subject before the verb → feminine plural verb.

Correct in Standard Arabic

دخلَ الطُلّابُ القاعةَ.

الطُلّابُ دخلوا القاعةَ.

Not correct in Standard Arabic

دخلوا الطُلّابُ القاعةَ.

Because the verb comes before the subject, the verb should be singular: دخلَ.

Quick examples

Verbal sentence Nominal sentence
كتبَ الطلابُ الواجبَ. الطلابُ كتبوا الواجبَ.
ذهبتْ النساءُ إلى الاجتماع. النساءُ ذهبنَ إلى الاجتماع.
وصلَ الضيوفُ مبكرًا. الضيوفُ وصلوا مبكرًا.
تغيّرتِ الخططُ بسرعة. الخططُ تغيّرتْ بسرعة.

A short rule to remember

Verb + subject = singular verb سافرَ الطلابُ.
Subject + verb = matching verb الطلابُ سافروا.

The verb in a verbal sentence remains singular in number, but it still reflects the gender of the subject: حضرَ الطلابُ / حضرتِ الطالباتُ.

Summary: In a verbal sentence, the verb comes before the subject and stays singular. In a nominal sentence, the subject comes before the verb, so the verb agrees with it in number and gender.

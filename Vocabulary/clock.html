In Arabic, time is expressed by the word الساعة (ʾas-sāʿa — literally the hour), followed by the number. Unlike English, Arabic uses a base-60 system similar to English but employs specific fraction words — ربع (quarter), نصف/نص (half), and ثلث/تلت (third/20 min) — rather than literal minute counts in everyday speech.

🏛️ Formal (Fus'ha)

Modern Standard Arabic. Used in news, official broadcasts, and writing. Distinctive for preserving case endings and specific fractional terms with "al-" (ال) attached.

🌿 Levantine

Spoken in Syria, Lebanon, Jordan, and Palestine. Uses وتلت (wa-tilt) for 20 minutes, drops case endings. Softer phonology.

🌙 Egyptian

Spoken in Egypt. Stresses geminated consonants (ونصّ), uses إلاّ for minutes before the hour. Uses literal numbers for 20 mins instead of "third".

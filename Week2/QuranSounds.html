Makharij al-Huruf (مخارج الحروف)

Understanding the Places of Articulation in Qur'anic Arabic

Introduction

In Tajweed, Makharij al-Huruf (مخارج الحروف) are the exact points of articulation for each Arabic letter. Mastering these ensures precise pronunciation, preserving the Qur'an's meaning and beauty.

There are five main articulation areas, leading to 17 specific points for the 29 Arabic letters. Explore them below:

1. Al-Jawf (الجوف) - The Empty Space / Oral Cavity

Al-Jawf is the empty space of the mouth and throat. It's the articulation point for the three long vowel letters (الحروف المدية - Huruf al-Madd), pronounced with a prolonged, airy sound without specific contact points.

  • Alif (ا): Preceded by a Fathah (َ).
  • Waw (و): Preceded by a Dhammah (ُ).
  • Ya (ي): Preceded by a Kasrah (ِ).

قَالَ إِنِّي أَنَا رَبُّكَ فَاخْلَعْ نَعْلَيْكَ ۖ إِنِّي بِالْوَادِ الْمُقَدَّسِ طُوًى
(Surah Taha, 20:12) - Long Waw

ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ ۛ فِيهِ ۛ هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ
(Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:2) - Long Ya

وَهُوَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ
(Surah Hud, 11:7) - Long Alif

2. Al-Halq (الحلق) - The Throat

The throat has three articulation points for six "Throat Letters" (الحروف الحلقية - Huruf al-Halq).

  • Deepest throat (أقصى الحلق): ء (Hamzah) and ه (Ha).
  • Middle throat (وسط الحلق): ع (Ain) and ح (Hha).
  • Closest throat (أدنى الحلق): غ (Ghain) and خ (Kha).

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
(Surah Al-Fatiha, 1:2) - Hha (ح)

إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ فِي لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ
(Surah Al-Qadr, 97:1) - Ha (ه)

غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ
(Surah Al-Fatiha, 1:7) - Ghain (غ)

3. Al-Khayshum (الخيشوم) - The Nasal Cavity

Al-Khayshum is the nasal cavity, producing the Ghunnah (غنة). This nasal sound accompanies ن (Noon) and م (Meem) with Shaddah (ّ) or in Tajweed rules like Ikhfa, Idgham, and Iqlaab. It's a two-count sound.

ثُمَّ إِنَّكُمْ أَيُّهَا الضَّالُّونَ الْمُكَذِّبُونَ
(Surah Al-Waqi'ah, 56:51) - Noon with Shaddah (إِنَّكُمْ)

وَلَمَّا جَاءَ أَمْرُنَا نَجَّيْنَا هُودًا وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مَعَهُ
(Surah Hud, 11:58) - Meem with Shaddah (وَلَمَّا)

4. Al-Lisan (اللسان) - The Tongue

The tongue is the most complex articulation area, producing 18 letters from 10 points. It's divided into four main sections:

The furthest part of the tongue towards the throat.

  • ق (Qaf): Deepest tongue with soft palate.
  • ك (Kaf): Slightly forward from Qaf, with hard palate.

قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ
(Surah Al-Ikhlas, 112:1) - Qaf (ق)

إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
(Surah Al-Fatiha, 1:5) - Kaf (ك)

The middle of the tongue articulates with the middle of the hard palate.

  • ج (Jeem), ش (Sheen), ي (Ya - non-madd): Share this point.

وَلَا تَقْرَبُوا الزِّنَا ۖ إِنَّهُ كَانَ فَاحِشَةً وَسَاءَ سَبِيلًا
(Surah Al-Isra, 17:32) - Sheen (ش)

وَقَالُوا اتَّخَذَ الرَّحْمَٰنُ وَلَدًا ۗ سُبْحَانَهُ ۚ بَلْ عِبَادٌ مُّكْرَمُونَ
(Surah Al-Anbiya, 21:26) - Jeem (ج)

The sides of the tongue articulate with the upper molars.

  • ض (Dhaad): Either or both sides of the tongue with upper molars (unique, challenging).
  • ل (Lam): Sides of the tongue, touching upper front teeth gums.

وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ
(Surah Al-Fatiha, 1:7) - Dhaad (ض)

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ
(Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:255) - Lam (ل)

The tip of the tongue is responsible for the largest group of letters, articulating with various parts of the upper gums and teeth.

  • ن (Noon): Tip with gum line of upper front teeth, with nasalization.
  • ر (Ra): Slightly behind Noon, tip with upper gum, slight trill.
  • ط (Tta), د (Dal), ت (Ta): Tip touching base of upper front teeth.
  • ص (Saad), ز (Zay), س (Seen): Tip near space between upper and lower front teeth (sibilant).
  • ظ (Dhaa), ذ (Dhal), ث (Tha): Tip touching tips of upper front teeth (interdental).

إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ
(Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:20) - Dal (د)

الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ
(Surah An-Nas, 114:5) - Noon (ن)

ثُمَّ سَوَّاهُ وَنَفَخَ فِيهِ مِن رُّوحِهِ
(Surah As-Sajdah, 32:9) - Tha (ث)

5. Ash-Shafataan (الشفاة) - The Two Lips

The lips articulate four letters from two points.

  • م (Meem) and ب (Ba): Both lips together. Meem includes Ghunnah.
  • و (Waw - non-madd): Lips rounded, not fully closed.
  • ف (Fa): Upper incisors on inner lower lip.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
(Surah Al-Fatiha, 1:1) - Ba (ب) and Meem (م)

وَأَنَّهُ هُوَ أَضْحَكَ وَأَبْكَىٰ
(Surah An-Najm, 53:43) - Waw (و)

فَإِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا
(Surah Al-Inshirah, 94:5) - Fa (ف)

1. Flashcards (40 Terms)

What is a **labeled training set**?
A dataset where all training examples include the desired solution (the label). Essential for **Supervised Learning**.
What is the difference between a **model parameter** and a **hyperparameter**?
**Parameter** (e.g., $m$ and $b$ in Linear Regression) is learned by the algorithm; **Hyperparameter** (e.g., Learning Rate) is set manually before training.
How does an **Instance-Based** learning algorithm make predictions?
It memorizes the training examples and uses a **similarity measure** to compare new instances to existing ones (e.g., k-Nearest Neighbors).
What is **Out-of-Core** learning, and when is it used?
A type of **Online Learning** used when the entire dataset is too large to fit in the computer's main memory (RAM). Data is loaded in chunks.
What is the **Generalization Error**?
The error rate on new, unseen cases (the **Test Set**). It measures how well the model generalizes from the training data to real-world data.
Name the two most common **Supervised Learning** tasks.
**Classification** (predicting a category) and **Regression** (predicting a continuous value).
Name four common **Unsupervised Learning** tasks.
Clustering, Visualization, Dimensionality Reduction, and Association Rule Learning.
What are the three main components of a **Reinforcement Learning** system?
**Agent** (the learner), **Environment**, and the **Policy** (the strategy the agent uses).
What is a key disadvantage of **Batch Learning**?
It requires vast computational resources and time to train, and retraining must occur on the entire dataset when new data arrives.
What is the primary goal of a **Model-Based** learning algorithm?
To search for the optimal values for model **parameters** that minimize a **cost function**.
In Reinforcement Learning, what is the **Utility Function** (or Reward Function)?
It measures how good the action of the agent was. The policy must be learned to maximize the expected reward over time.
What happens when a model is **Underfitting** the training data?
The model is too simple to capture the underlying structure, resulting in poor performance on both training and test data.
What is the problem known as **Data Snooping**?
Tuning hyperparameters based on the test set, which leads to the test set error being too optimistic (the model overfits the test set).
What is the role of the **Validation Set** in the holdout method?
It is used to evaluate candidate models and select the best hyperparameters without touching the final Test Set.
What does **Feature Engineering** involve?
Selecting the most useful features, transforming features, and creating new features by combining existing ones.
Name two problems related to **Bad Training Data**.
Insufficient quantity of training data and non-representative training data (**Sampling Bias**).
What type of ML is **Association Rule Learning**?
Unsupervised Learning (e.g., finding that people who buy product A also tend to buy product B).
What is a key cause of **Overfitting**?
The model is too complex relative to the amount or noise of the training data, learning the "noise" and not just the signal.
Describe **Semi-Supervised Learning**.
Algorithms that handle partially labeled data, often by clustering the unlabeled data first and then using the few labels to refine the clusters.
What is the role of the **Cost Function**?
Measures how far the model's prediction is from the desired output. The goal of training is to minimize this value.
How do **Online Learning** systems update their model?
They train the model incrementally, either individually or in small groups (mini-batches), allowing it to adapt to new data instantly.
What is the main difference in how **Instance-Based** vs. **Model-Based** systems generalize?
Instance-based generalizes by comparing new data to memorized instances. Model-based generalizes by finding optimal parameters for an explicit model.
Name three solutions for **Overfitting**.
Simplify the model, use more training data, or reduce the noise in the data (e.g., regularization).
What is **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)**?
A technique for **Dimensionality Reduction** that projects the data onto a lower-dimensional subspace while preserving variance.
What type of ML problem is a **Spam Filter**?
Supervised Learning, specifically **Binary Classification** (Spam or Not Spam).
What happens to old data in a production **Online Learning** system?
The old data can often be discarded after a training step, saving massive amounts of storage space.
What is the sole purpose of the **Training Set**?
To train the model by finding the optimal parameters that minimize the cost function on this data.
When is a **Train-Dev Set** needed?
When there is a significant mismatch between the training data and the validation/test data (e.g., training on clean data, testing on noisy data).
What is the "Garbage In, Garbage Out" challenge in ML?
The model will only be as good as the data it is trained on. Poor quality features (missing, noisy, irrelevant) lead to poor performance.
Training with a set that is too small to generalize is called **Sampling Noise**. What is training with a biased sample called?
What is the difference between **Sampling Noise** and **Sampling Bias**?
In Reinforcement Learning, what is the **Policy**?
It is the strategy that the agent uses to decide what actions to take in a given situation. It's the "brain" of the agent.
What is the most common strategy Model-Based algorithms use to succeed?
Iteratively adjusting the parameters to minimize the cost function using an algorithm like **Gradient Descent**.
Why is **Visualization** considered an Unsupervised Task?
It's a way to present complex data in a 2D or 3D plot to allow a human observer to spot patterns, and it requires no labels.
What is the ML challenge related to **Extrapolating** beyond the training data?
The model may perform poorly when asked to predict values far outside the range it was trained on, which is a key danger of non-representative data.
What type of ML is used for **Creating a chatbot**?
Typically a mixture of Supervised Learning (for classification of intent) and sometimes Reinforcement Learning (to optimize conversation flow).
What is a technique for handling noise/outliers in data for Instance-Based Learning?
Instead of using the nearest neighbor (k=1), use k-Nearest Neighbors with a larger k, so the prediction is based on the majority vote or average of k neighbors.
What is the **Learning Rate** hyperparameter?
It controls the size of the steps taken by the optimization algorithm (e.g., Gradient Descent) when adjusting model parameters.
What is the idea behind **Ensemble Learning**?
Combining the predictions of several different models (e.g., using a consensus) to achieve better performance than any single model alone.
What is the issue of **Data Mismatch**?
The training data is drawn from a different distribution than the validation/test data, leading to poor generalization despite good training performance.
Why is using the Test Set for hyperparameter tuning a bad idea?
It violates the principle that the test set should be a final, unbiased measure of the model's true generalization error.

3. True or False (30 Statements)

1. The problem of spam detection is typically framed as a Supervised Learning problem.

True. Spam filters are trained on labeled examples (Spam/Not Spam), making it a supervised classification task.

2. If a model performs great on the training data but poorly on the test set, it is suffering from **Underfitting**.

False. This scenario describes **Overfitting**, where the model learns the noise in the training data too well and fails to generalize.

3. The primary purpose of the **Validation Set** is to tune hyperparameters and select the best model.

True. The validation set is used for the holdout validation process to prevent overfitting the test set.

4. Regression is the task of predicting a discrete, categorical value.

False. Regression predicts a **continuous** value. Classification predicts a discrete, categorical value.

5. Batch learning systems must be trained using all available data at once.

True. Batch learning involves a single, resource-intensive training step using all data.

6. An Instance-Based learning algorithm attempts to explicitly find the optimal parameters of a function.

False. This is the goal of **Model-Based** learning. Instance-Based learning relies on similarity measures.

7. If you tune hyperparameters using the test set, you risk a phenomenon called Data Snooping.

True. Data Snooping leads to an overly optimistic estimate of the generalization error because the model has indirectly "seen" the test data.

8. A good solution to underfitting is to use a less constrained model with more parameters.

True. Underfitting means the model is too simple. A more powerful (more complex) model is needed.

9. Online learning models are often used when data must be processed sequentially as it arrives.

True. This is ideal for continuous data streams like stock prices or customer clicks.

10. Feature Scaling (e.g., Normalization) is a key challenge when working with Instance-Based Learning models.

True. Instance-Based Learning relies on similarity/distance measures, which are heavily affected by feature scales.

11. Clustering is an example of a Supervised Learning task.

False. Clustering is an **Unsupervised** Learning task because the data has no labels.

12. Out-of-core learning is a technique used only for very small datasets.

False. It is used when the dataset is **too large** to fit into the computer's main memory (RAM).

13. A Model Parameter is determined by the training algorithm itself, not set manually.

True. Model parameters (like weights) are the outcome of the learning process, unlike hyperparameters (like learning rate).

14. Using a training set that is not representative of the real-world data is known as **Sampling Bias**.

True. This bias leads to poor generalization because the model learns patterns that don't reflect reality.

15. The Generalization Error is typically measured on the Training Set.

False. It is measured on the **Test Set** (or out-of-sample data).

16. If the training data distribution differs significantly from the test data distribution, you should consider using a **train-dev set**.

True. The train-dev set helps diagnose if the high generalization error is due to overfitting or data mismatch.

17. Ensemble methods combine multiple models to create a more robust final prediction.

True. Ensemble Learning is a powerful technique for boosting performance, especially by reducing variance (overfitting).

18. In Reinforcement Learning, the **Agent** is the environment that provides rewards and penalties.

False. The Agent is the **learning system**. The **Environment** provides the rewards/penalties.

19. For simple problems, most ML algorithms need millions of data points to perform well.

False. For simple problems, only thousands of data points may be sufficient. Millions are typically needed for complex problems like image recognition.

20. Google Photos' face grouping feature is a prime example of pure Supervised Learning.

False. Face grouping uses **Clustering** (Unsupervised) to group faces, then asks for a name label (Supervised). This makes it **Semi-Supervised**.

21. Feature Engineering is about automatically selecting the best features; it rarely involves human intervention.

False. Feature Engineering requires considerable **human expertise** to select, combine, and create new features.

22. Outliers generally have a negligible impact on most ML models.

False. Outliers can severely damage model performance, especially in models sensitive to distance like Linear Regression and kNN.

23. Applying regularization (constraining the model) is a common strategy to combat overfitting.

True. By constraining the model, we reduce its complexity, forcing it to focus on general patterns rather than noise.

24. It is generally safe to rely on an ML model to extrapolate (predict outside its training range).

False. Extrapolation is notoriously unreliable and should be avoided, as the model has no data to validate its prediction in that range.

25. The Policy in RL is the strategy for the agent to maximize the utility function.

True. The Policy dictates the agent's actions based on the current state, aiming for the maximum cumulative reward.

26. Unlike batch learning, online learning requires retraining on all previous data every time new data is introduced.

False. Online learning's strength is that it learns incrementally, often discarding old data immediately, thus avoiding full retraining.

27. A high training error always indicates that the model is overfitting.

False. A high training error usually indicates **underfitting** (the model isn't even learning the training data well). Overfitting has low training error but high test error.

28. The main difficulty in scaling up ML is the exponential increase in the cost of collecting more data.

False. While collecting data is hard, the main scaling difficulty is the **computational complexity** of training the model on huge datasets (solved partially by Online/Out-of-Core learning).

29. If your model performs poorly, adding more features is always the first and best solution.

False. Adding poor or irrelevant features can make things worse (Garbage In, Garbage Out). Improving existing features (Feature Engineering) or increasing data size is often better.

30. The goal of Visualization techniques is to transform data into a clear 2D or 3D representation for human interpretation.

True. Visualization, an Unsupervised task, is primarily for human insight into the data's structure.

4. Multiple Choice Questions (30 Questions)

1. Which of the following is NOT one of the two most common Supervised Learning tasks?

Explanation: Clustering is a prominent Unsupervised Learning task, while Classification and Regression are the two main Supervised tasks.

2. What is the primary goal of a **Model-Based** learning algorithm?

Explanation: Model-based learning, like Linear Regression, is about finding the optimal parameters (weights/slopes) that fit the data best according to a cost function.

3. If your model is suffering from **Underfitting**, which is the BEST general solution to try?

Explanation: Underfitting means the model is too simple for the data. Using a more complex or powerful model (like adding polynomial features) allows it to capture more complex patterns.

4. Which of the following best describes a **hyperparameter**?

Explanation: Hyperparameters (like the learning rate or regularization strength) are external settings that control the learning process, distinct from model parameters (like weights/biases) which are learned.

5. Which technique is most effective at preventing a model from **overfitting**?

Explanation: Regularization (like L1/L2 constraints) is a standard method to simplify the model and reduce overfitting, forcing it to generalize better.

6. What is a key advantage of **Online Learning** over Batch Learning?

Explanation: Online learning processes data incrementally (adaptability) and can handle data that is too large for memory (out-of-core).

7. Which type of Unsupervised Learning is most appropriate for grouping similar data points without any prior knowledge of group labels?

Explanation: Clustering algorithms discover inherent groupings in the data without relying on pre-existing labels.

8. If you repeatedly tune your model's hyperparameters using the **Test Set**, the final generalization error estimate will likely be:

Explanation: This is Data Snooping. By tuning to the Test Set, the model effectively overfits it, resulting in an error estimate that is better than the model's true performance on completely new data.

9. An algorithm that uses a **similarity measure** (like distance) to make predictions by comparing a new instance to memorized training data is called:

Explanation: Instance-Based Learning, like kNN, keeps all instances and generalizes only when a prediction is needed.

10. Predicting a house price far outside the range of prices seen in the training data is an example of **extrapolation**. Why is this dangerous?

Explanation: Extrapolation assumes the patterns learned within the training range continue indefinitely, which is often false in the real world.

11. What is the key distinction between **Clustering** and **Classification**?

Explanation: This is the fundamental difference: Classification uses labels to map inputs to outputs (S), while Clustering finds patterns in unlabeled data (U).

12. Which of the following is NOT a strategy to fix an **Underfitting** model?

Explanation: Decreasing data size would likely make the model less capable and could exacerbate underfitting (or lead to overfitting if the data is too small). The other options help increase model complexity or provide more signal.

13. In Reinforcement Learning, the learning system is called the **Agent**. What does it learn to maximize?

Explanation: The agent learns a Policy that guides its actions to achieve the highest cumulative reward in the environment.

14. When practicing **Holdout Validation**, what is the primary purpose of the **Validation Set**?

Explanation: The Validation Set is the "development" set, used for internal model selection and hyperparameter tuning before the final Test Set.

15. The main challenge of training with **non-representative data** is:

Explanation: Sampling bias (non-representative data) is a core problem leading to poor generalization, as the model learns irrelevant patterns.

16. What is the major drawback of **Batch Learning** when new data needs to be added?

Explanation: Batch learning's key drawback is the need for full retraining, a time- and resource-intensive process. Catastrophic forgetting is an issue for Online Learning.

17. Which unsupervised task is typically used to simplify datasets for better human understanding?

Explanation: Both Visualization and Dimensionality Reduction aim to present or reduce data complexity for human inspection.

18. To address a problem where the model is performing poorly due to high variance (overfitting), you could:

Explanation: Overfitting means the model is learning noise. More data or simplifying the model (fewer features) forces it to learn only the main patterns.

19. Which Machine Learning approach is defined by an **Agent** observing an **Environment**, selecting and performing **Actions**, and receiving **Rewards**?

Explanation: This describes the core mechanism of Reinforcement Learning, where the Agent learns the optimal Policy through interaction.

20. Which challenge in Machine Learning is captured by the phrase "Garbage In, Garbage Out"?

Explanation: This phrase emphasizes that a model's performance is fundamentally limited by the quality and relevance of its input data.

21. To solve a complex problem like large-scale image classification, an algorithm typically needs:

Explanation: Complex models for high-dimensional data (like images) require massive amounts of labeled data to learn meaningful generalizations.

22. What is a common technique used in **Semi-Supervised Learning**?

Explanation: This "label propagation" or "self-training" approach is central to Semi-Supervised methods like self-training models or deep generative models.

23. Which action would generally **reduce** the complexity of a model, thereby addressing overfitting?

Explanation: Increasing regularization strength penalizes large parameter values, effectively simplifying the model and reducing its capacity to overfit.

24. What is the alternative term for the **Generalization Error**?

Explanation: Generalization error specifically refers to the error on data the model has not seen before, hence "out-of-sample" error.

25. When would you absolutely need to use an **Out-of-Core** learning technique?

Explanation: Out-of-core learning is specifically designed for datasets too large to fit in RAM, relying on feeding the data in chunks (Online Learning).

26. Which action is the most direct way to mitigate the challenge of **Sampling Bias**?

Explanation: Sampling Bias is a data quality issue; it is solved by improving the sampling method and ensuring representation of all important subgroups.

27. If a model has a training error of 1% and a test error of 15%, the model most likely suffers from:

Explanation: A large gap between low training error and high test error is the classic sign of **overfitting** (high variance).

28. Which of the following is considered part of the **Feature Engineering** process?

Explanation: Feature Engineering is the art of transforming raw data into features that best represent the underlying problem to the model.

29. What component in Reinforcement Learning dictates how the agent should behave in a given situation?

Explanation: The Policy is the agent's strategy or algorithm for choosing actions.

30. What is the key difference that separates **Supervised** and **Unsupervised** learning?

Explanation: The presence of labels (target values) is the single defining characteristic dividing supervised from unsupervised learning.

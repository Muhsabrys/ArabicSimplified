Notes on vocabulary usage

Open each note to review the distinction and examples.

3 notes
1. “Pants” and “shoes” in Arabic

“Pants” and “shoes” are plural in English, but Arabic usually refers to one pair of either item with the singular form.

في العالَم العربي، مُعظَمُ الرِّجالِ لا يَلْبَسونَ البَنْطَلونَ القَصيرَ في الشّارِع.

كِنْدَرْتِك حِلْوِة كْتير!

الجَزْمَة دي واسْعَة عَلَيَّ!

2. شَخْص versus النّاس

شَخْص refers to a specific person, specific people, or a specific number of people. Use النّاس for people in general.

حَضَرَ سَبْعَةُ أَشْخاصٍ فَقَط إلى العَمَلِ اليَوْمَ بِسَبَبِ الثَّلْج.

مُعْظَمُ النّاسِ يَخافونَ مِن حُكوماتِهِم أَحْيانًا.

3. اِنْتَشَرَ is intransitive

اِنْتَشَرَ does not take a direct object. Use it for something that spreads by itself.

اِنْتَشَرَتِ الأَخْبارُ بِسُرْعَة.

To say “They spread the news,” use a transitive verb from another pattern:

نَشَروا الأَخْبارَ.

These words are closely related in form and meaning to present-tense verbs from Forms II–X. They typically begin with مُـ and have a kasra in the final syllable. In traditional terminology, many of these are active participles.

Do not confuse them with every word beginning with م. For example, مَلابِس begins with مَـ, not مُـ, and is not derived by this rule.

Open response

Where do they buy their clothes? Which clothes do they wear constantly? Do they prefer small shops or big stores? Why?

What do they intend to do after completing their studies? In which year do they expect to finish? Do they intend to stay in this city or move elsewhere?

Should the university permit students to travel wherever in the world they want to study? Why or why not?

How do they know if a person has a good personality? What do they look for in a person?

What do they think of people who get angry a lot? Is this problem widespread? Why? Is there a solution?

Which immigrant communities live in this city? Is the number of immigrants increasing? Are immigrants spread out or concentrated in one area? What shops or businesses do they have?

Should there be limits on freedom of speech? If so, who should determine them?
